## About the Business
Kay-Wi Limited is a construction and inspection company building hostel-style apartment projects near the Nigerian Law School in Abuja. Their target audience is international students. They need end-to-end property operations technology — from student onboarding and identity verification to booking, payment, access control, and facility management.

## Client Pain Points (from discovery meetings)
Direct quotes and concerns from Kay-Wi during meetings:
- "We want to reduce administrative workload."
- "Bookings and payments should be seamless."
- "Management should not be stressful for staff."
- "How can technology manage this end-to-end?"

Specific operational concerns:
- High administrative workload running student accommodation
- Manual booking processes
- Payment inefficiencies
- Access control issues (physical keys for multiple units)
- Facility tracking needs (gas, electricity, maintenance items)

## Solution Proposed

### Spacer Core Platform — NGN 11,000,000
- Spacer Core Customization — booking system, KYC/ID verification, access automation
- Access Control Logic — QR + smart lock orchestration and lifecycle management
- Deployment & Configuration — core system setup, configuration, and go-live support

**How it works for Kay-Wi:**
1. Student visits Spacer booking link
2. Uploads ID for verification before payment is allowed
3. Selects room and pays online
4. Receives automated access credentials (QR code / access code) — time-bound, expires at end of stay
5. Credentials auto-deactivate after stay expiry
6. All bookings, payments, and access managed from admin dashboard

### Facility Management Module — NGN 16,000,000
- Electricity tracking and management
- Gas supply monitoring
- Outage tracking and reporting
- Maintenance request and resolution tracking
- Facility dashboards for management visibility

*(Originally proposed at NGN 20,000,000. Reduced to NGN 16,000,000 during price negotiation — a strategic concession to secure the long-term enterprise client while maintaining core Spacer pricing.)*

### Gym Subscription System — NGN 3,000,000
- Resident and external gym access management
- Subscription payments and processing
- Expiry logic and automated renewals
- Access control for gym facility

### Network & Systems Setup — NGN 6,400,000
- Managed PoE Network Switches (8 units × NGN 350,000) — centralised switching for access devices
- Structured Cabling & Termination (4 units × NGN 600,000) — cabling, termination, and labelling
- Network Configuration & VLAN Setup (NGN 1,200,000) — segmentation, routing, and traffic prioritisation

## How Kay-Wi Was Won (Acquisition Blueprint)
This was NOT a cold sale. It was a deployment-driven enterprise discovery:

1. **Live product exposure** — Kay-Wi saw Spacer being deployed at Saadatu's Apartments during a building inspection
2. **Organic curiosity** — "What system is this?" — not pitched, discovered
3. **Credibility through deployment** — Algolog wasn't selling slides, they were installing a real system in a real building
4. **Consultative solution sale** — meetings focused on Kay-Wi's specific needs, not generic product features
5. **Relationship nurturing** — Yasmin maintained ongoing communication during proposal development and internal discussion phases, preventing deal drop-off
6. **Detailed, customised proposal** — covered Spacer, facility management, networking, automation workflows, and financial projections specific to Kay-Wi's building model
7. **Strategic price concession** — NGN 4M reduction on facility management to secure the enterprise client while protecting core pricing

This is Algolog's replicable model: **Deploy at Property A → Get discovered by Company B → Consultative sale → Enterprise conversion.**
