### Page 2: The Occupancy Blind Spot

Most operators think their occupancy is higher than it actually is. Why? Because they remember the busy weekends. They remember the sold-out Friday nights and the back-to-back Saturday bookings. What they don't remember — or don't track — is the empty Tuesday. The vacant Wednesday. The room that sat unbooked for four consecutive weeknights.

The reality is this: most 10-unit serviced apartment properties in Nigeria operate at 50-65% occupancy. That means 35-50% of their room-nights generate zero revenue.

Here's the thing that makes this painful: you're paying rent, electricity, DSTV, cleaning, and security on every room, every day — whether a guest is in it or not. An empty room doesn't cost nothing. It costs everything except the revenue it was supposed to generate.

**Yellow Callout — What it's costing you:**
[Total rooms] x [Empty nights per month] x [Your nightly rate]

For a 10-unit property with 35% vacancy at NGN 30,000/night:
- That's 105 empty room-nights per month
- = NGN 3.15 million in potential revenue sitting on the table every single month

This isn't a theoretical number. It's the gap between what your property could earn and what it actually earns. And the first step to closing that gap is knowing the gap exists.

### Page 3: Know Your Real Number — The Occupancy Formula

**The formula:**
(Total Booked Room-Nights / Total Available Room-Nights) x 100 = Occupancy Rate

**Worked example — 10-unit property, 30-day month:**
- Total available room-nights: 10 x 30 = 300
- If 195 were booked: 195 / 300 = 65% occupancy
- That means 105 room-nights earned nothing

**RevPAR — Revenue Per Available Room:**
Total Room Revenue / Total Available Room-Nights = RevPAR

Example:
- If you earned NGN 5.85M from those 195 booked nights
- RevPAR = NGN 5,850,000 / 300 = NGN 19,500
- Your nightly rate is NGN 30,000 — but you're only making NGN 19,500 per room per night when you account for the empty ones

**Key insight:** RevPAR is the number that tells you the truth. Your nightly rate is what you charge. RevPAR is what you actually earn.

Most operators focus on their nightly rate and ignore RevPAR. But RevPAR is the number that determines whether your property is profitable or just busy on weekends.

### Page 4: The 3 Biggest Causes of Invisible Vacancy

**Cause 1: No midweek strategy**
Weekends tend to fill themselves — especially in cities like Abuja and Kaduna where short-stay demand spikes Friday through Sunday. But Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday? Those nights consistently underperform. Most operators don't even track which days of the week are weakest. They just accept that "midweek is always slow" without doing anything targeted about it.

**Cause 2: No gap-fill pricing**
A room sitting empty tonight at NGN 30,000 will earn NGN 0 tomorrow. A room sold tonight at NGN 20,000 earns NGN 20,000. The maths is simple, but many operators would rather leave a room empty than "discount" it. There's a psychological resistance to lowering prices — it feels like devaluing the property. But an empty room costs money (rent, electricity, cleaning, security). A discounted room makes money. The real devaluation is leaving revenue on the table.

**Cause 3: No visibility into patterns**
Without data, you can't see that Room 7 has been empty every Monday through Wednesday for the last 3 months. You can't see that your occupancy drops 40% the week after salary week. You can't see that your best-performing unit is carried by repeat guests while your worst-performing unit hasn't had a booking in 12 days. You can't act on patterns you can't see.

### Page 5: The Free Fix — Do This Today
*Give away the secret — a printable weekly occupancy tracker*

**Step 1:** Create a simple table or spreadsheet with columns:
| Week | Room 1 | Room 2 | Room 3 | ... | Room 10 | Total Booked | Total Available | Occupancy % |

Mark each cell with a checkmark (booked) or leave blank (empty) for each night.

**Step 2:** Fill it in every Friday afternoon. It takes 10 minutes.

**Step 3:** After 4 weeks, you'll have your real monthly occupancy rate — not a guess, not a feeling, but an actual number.

**Step 4:** After 8 weeks, you'll see patterns:
- Which rooms consistently underperform
- Which days of the week are weakest
- Which weeks of the month dip

**Action steps once you see the data:**
- Midweek occupancy below 40%? Create a midweek promo (3-night stay, pay for 2)
- Same rooms always empty? Check pricing, check listing quality, consider different marketing for those specific units
- Post-salary-week dip? Pre-book loyal customers with a standing reservation offer

**Orange callout — Honest note:**
Tracking by hand gives you the data. But it's backward-looking — you see what happened, not what's happening now. And it can't alert you to opportunities in real time. By the time you notice Room 3 has been empty all week, the week is already over. A manual tracker tells you where the problem was. Automation tells you where the opportunity is — right now.
