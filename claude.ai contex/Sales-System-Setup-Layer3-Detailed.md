# Sales System Setup — Layer 3 Detailed Items

## 1. Define core offer using Value Equation

**The Value Equation (Hormozi):**

```
              Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
Value = ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
              Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice
```

**Increase value by:** maximizing the top (dream outcome + likelihood) and minimizing the bottom (time + effort).

**Algolog SDS Core Offer — Applied:**

| Element | What It Means | How We Deliver |
|---------|--------------|----------------|
| **Dream Outcome** | The client's business runs smoother, makes more money, and they stop doing manual work that drains their time | We build systems that automate what they're doing manually — bookings, payments, reconciliation, reporting, access control. They go from WhatsApp and Excel to a dashboard that runs itself. |
| **Perceived Likelihood** | The client believes this will actually work for THEM, not just in theory | Live demos on real deployed systems (Spacer at 4 properties). Show it working NOW, not a mockup. Testimonials from similar businesses. "We built this for a 12-unit apartment in Kaduna — here's what it looks like live." |
| **Time Delay** | How long before they see results | Spacer deployments: 2-4 weeks to live. SDS custom builds: 6-8 weeks. We frame it as "your system is processing bookings within a month" — not "the project takes 3 months." |
| **Effort & Sacrifice** | How much work does the CLIENT have to do | Minimal. We handle setup, configuration, training. They provide content/data, we do everything else. "Your team gets trained, you get a manual, and the system runs." No IT team needed on their side. |

**The Offer Statement (use in every pitch):**

> "We build the system, we deploy it, we train your team, and we support it. Within [timeframe], your [process] is automated — bookings come in, payments are confirmed, reports are generated, and you stop doing it manually. If it's not working, we fix it until it does."

| Price Range | Format | Details | Our Products |
|------------|--------|---------|-------------|
| **High-ticket (>NGN 1M)** | Human sales call — face-to-face or Zoom | Full Diagnose → Position → Handle Objections → Close process. 30-60 min meeting. Proposal presented in person or on call, NOT emailed. | All SDS builds (StarbuckTrack, Court474, WFG), All Spacer deployments |
| **Mid-ticket (NGN 100K-1M)** | Solution development call + sent proposal | Shorter discovery. 15-20 min call. Proposal follows same day. Close on follow-up call. | Smaller add-ons, single-module deployments, consulting engagements |
| **Recurring (subscription)** | Trial + onboarding | Show the product working. Get them using it. Value sells itself. | Pledged (church signs up → members self-serve), future SaaS products |

**All current Algolog SDS deals are high-ticket.** Every deal requires a human conversation.

**The rule:** The higher the price, the more human the interaction must be. Never try to close a multi-million naira deal over email or with a PDF alone. The rep IS the product at high-ticket.

**Format by stage:**

| Stage | What Happens | Format |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| **Setter call (Faridah)** | Books the meeting. Does NOT sell. Identifies pain, confirms interest, schedules. | Phone/WhatsApp — 5-10 min |
| **Discovery meeting** | Diagnose. Understand business, find hot button, identify decision-makers. | Face-to-face at office or Zoom — 30-45 min |
| **Proposal presentation** | Position + Close. Present solution tied to their words. Ask for the order. | Face-to-face or Zoom — 30 min. NEVER just email the proposal. |
| **Follow-up / Objection handling** | Handle "think about it", "check with partner", price concerns. | Call/WhatsApp/visit — as many touches as needed |
| **Contract + Invoice** | Close. Send agreement + invoice. Get signature + payment. | Email with follow-up call same day |

## 3. Write sales script: Diagnose → Position → Handle Objections → Close

### DIAGNOSE (5-10 minutes)

**Opening (set the frame):**
> "Thank you for your time. Before I show you anything, I'd love to understand your business first — what you do, how things currently work, and where you feel there's room to improve. All I ask is that at the end, you tell me honestly whether what I show you makes sense or not. Is that fair?"

**Discovery questions (in order):**

1. "Tell me about your business — what does your day-to-day look like?"
2. "What's the biggest challenge you're dealing with right now?"
3. "How are you currently handling [the process they just described]?"
4. "On a scale of 1-10, how would you rate your current setup?"
5. "What would make it a 10?"
6. "How long have you been dealing with this?"
7. "What happens if nothing changes in the next 6-12 months?"
8. "Who else would be involved in a decision like this?"

**What to listen for:**
- The ONE thing they care about most (the hot button) — usually revealed in answer to question 2 or 5
- Their exact words describing the problem — write them down, use them back in Step 2
- Emotional language: "frustrated", "stressed", "always on-site", "can't sleep" — these are buying signals
- Who the real decision-maker is (question 8)

**Rules:**
- Do NOT pitch yet. Just listen and ask follow-ups.
- "How do you mean?" — use this after any statement you want them to expand on
- Keep notes. Their words become your positioning script.

---

### POSITION (10-15 minutes)

**Transition from Diagnose:**
> "Thank you for sharing that. Based on what you've told me — [repeat their top 2-3 pain points in their own words] — let me show you how we'd solve this."

**Present using the hot button framework:**
1. Lead with the ONE benefit they care about most
2. Use "Because of this... you can... which means..." for each feature
3. Show, don't just tell — live demo on phone, printed materials, real data
4. Trial close after each benefit: "Does this make sense so far?" / "Would this help?"

**Example (Spacer for hotel owner who said "I'm always on-site"):**
> "You mentioned you're always on-site because you have to be there for every check-in and key handover. Because Spacer generates unique access codes for each guest — tied to their exact stay dates — you can eliminate that entirely. Guests get their code by SMS, they let themselves in, the code expires at checkout. Which means you don't have to be on-site for arrivals anymore. Would that be valuable to you?"

**If they say yes → move toward close. Don't keep presenting more features.**

**Positioning by buyer type (Tracy — 4 main types):**

| Type (25% each) | How to Spot Them | How to Present |
|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Analytical** | Detail-focused, slow, asks lots of questions. Accounting, engineering, IT. | Prove it on paper. Data, numbers, comparisons. Don't rush. |
| **Relator** | People-oriented, checks with everyone, wants to be liked. HR, teaching, nursing. | Build the relationship first. Show who else uses it. Testimonials matter most. |
| **Driver** | Direct, impatient, bottom-line. Entrepreneurs, execs. | Speed up. Skip small talk. Lead with ROI. "Here's what it does, here's what it costs." |
| **Socializer** | Outgoing, talks about achievements, may agree too quickly. | Let them talk. Write everything down (they may forget what they agreed to). Get commitment in writing. |

### HANDLE OBJECTIONS (as they come)

**The universal first response to ANY objection:**
> "How do you mean?"

Then listen. They'll clarify and often soften their own objection.

**After they clarify, use the AGREE → REDIRECT → CLOSE pattern:**

1. **Agree:** "You're absolutely right." / "I understand." / "That's a fair point."
2. **Redirect:** Pivot back to their hot button or address the real concern
3. **Close:** Ask for the order again

**Quick reference:**

| They Say | You Say |
|----------|---------|
| "Too expensive" | "You're right, it's a significant investment. You mentioned you're losing NGN [X] per month from [problem]. This eliminates that. The system pays for itself in [timeframe]." If still resisting: move UP to bigger package, or remove a feature — never discount. |
| "Let me think about it" | "You said you'd tell me honestly. From everything you've shared, this seems like exactly what you need — unless there's something I'm not understanding?" Then silence. |
| "Need to check with partner/board" | "That makes sense. What if I joined that meeting? Even 15 minutes — so they can ask me directly instead of you having to relay." |
| "We have something already" | "Great. On a scale of 1-10, how would you rate it? ... What would make it a 10?" |
| "Can you send a proposal?" | "I'd love to. I just need 15 minutes to understand your setup so the proposal hits the mark. Would Thursday or Friday work?" |
| "Not the right time" | "I understand. If you were to move forward with something like this in the future, what would cause you to?" Then silence — they reveal the real objection. |
| "Competitor is cheaper" | "You should explore your options. If theirs genuinely works, go with them. What I can tell you is — I come with this transaction. If something breaks at 9pm, you're calling me, not a helpdesk." |
| "We're too small for this" | "One of our clients in Kaduna has 12 units — smaller than you. She was on WhatsApp. Within two weeks, her guests were booking and paying online automatically. She got her evenings back." |

---

### CLOSE (2-5 minutes)

**When to close:** As soon as they've shown positive response to the hot button. Don't wait until you've presented everything.

**The close sequence:**

**Close #1 — The Direct Ask:**
> "Based on everything we've discussed, I think this is exactly what you need. Can we move forward?"

*Silence. Wait for response.*

**If soft resistance → Close #2 — The Assumptive:**
> "When we deploy this for you, the first thing your team will notice is [hot button benefit]. Let me walk you through the next steps."

**If "need to think" → Close #3 — The Approach Callback:**
> "You said you'd tell me honestly. After everything you've shared, this seems ideal — unless there's something else?"

**If budget concern → Close #4 — The Phased Start:**
> "What if we started with [core module] first? That addresses your biggest pain point immediately. We phase in the rest once you're seeing value."

**If risk concern → Close #5 — The Checkpoint:**
> "Let's deploy and build in a 90-day review. At 90 days, we look at the numbers together. If it's not delivering, we figure out what to adjust."

**After they say yes:**
> "Great. Here's what happens next — I'll send you the agreement and invoice today. Once the upfront payment is confirmed, we begin setup within [timeframe]. I'll be your point of contact throughout."

**Rules:**
- Ask for the close at least 3-5 times. Most sales happen after the 5th ask.
- After every ask — silence. "The buying takes place in the silence."
- Never leave a meeting without a clear next step.

If the system isn't operational within 30 days of receiving your content and property details, we continue working at no additional cost until it is.

### SDS Custom Builds
> **The Delivery Guarantee:** We deliver the agreed scope within the stated timeline. If we miss the delivery date (and the delay is not caused by content or information delays from the client), we continue development at no additional cost until delivery is complete.

### All Projects
> **The Support Guarantee:** After deployment, we provide ongoing support for any issues within the originally agreed scope. If something breaks, we fix it. No support fees, no ticket queues — you call us directly.

**How to present the guarantee in meetings:**
> "Here's what I want you to know — if we deploy this and it's not working as described within [timeframe], we keep working until it does. At no additional cost. We don't walk away from a deployment. That's not how we operate."

**Rules:**
- State the guarantee BEFORE presenting price — it lowers the perceived risk, making price easier to accept
- The guarantee should feel confident, not desperate. You're not begging — you're showing conviction.
- Never guarantee results you can't control (e.g., "we guarantee your revenue will double" — that depends on their market, not your software)

## 6. Create urgency mechanism (real, not manufactured)

### 1. Cost of Inaction (use in every meeting)
> "Every month this stays manual, you're spending [X hours / losing X naira / risking X errors]. That cost is already happening — the question is how many more months you want to absorb it."

Calculate it with them in the meeting:
- "You said reconciliation takes 4 hours every week. That's 16 hours a month. At what rate? That's NGN [X] per month in staff time alone — not counting the errors."
- "You said giving drops 40% during holidays. If your average Sunday is NGN [X], that's NGN [Y] lost over Easter, Christmas, and summer travel."

This is not manufactured — it's math they can verify.

### 2. Implementation Queue (when true)
> "We're currently working on [X number] of deployments. If we start this month, we can have you live by [date]. If it pushes to next month, the timeline extends because of our current commitments."

Only use this when it's genuinely true. Never fabricate scarcity.

### 3. Seasonal Timing (for specific industries)
- **Hotels/apartments:** "Peak booking season is [month]. If we deploy now, you're capturing those bookings digitally. If you wait, that's another peak season on WhatsApp."
- **Churches (Pledged):** "Easter / Christmas is [X weeks away]. If we set up now, you capture the holiday giving. If you wait, that's another holiday season where giving drops 40%."
- **Oil & gas:** "End of quarter is [date]. If the system is live before then, your quarterly reconciliation is automated. Otherwise, it's another manual cycle."

### 4. Phased Deployment with Early Start
> "We can start the setup and configuration now while you finalize the payment terms. That way you're not losing time. By the time the agreement is signed, we're already halfway through setup."

**Rules:**
- NEVER manufacture fake urgency ("this price is only available today"). Algolog's tone is warm and professional — not high-pressure.
- ALWAYS use the cost of inaction — it's real, specific to them, and the most powerful urgency lever.
