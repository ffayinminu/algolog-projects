## About the Business
Dalema is a boutique-style supermarket in Maitama, Abuja — one of the most affluent neighbourhoods in Nigeria. Known for carrying foreign brands, specialty items (organic, gluten-free, vegan), and a premium bakery/deli section with freshly baked goods prepared daily by in-house chefs.

**Key characteristics:**
- Premium positioning — not a mass-market supermarket, more upscale/boutique
- International food section with specialty items you won't find elsewhere
- In-store bakery and deli (freshly baked bread, pastries, sandwiches daily)
- Two locations (Abuja Maitama + Kaduna)
- Instagram: @dalema_ng
- Operating hours: Mon-Sat 8am-10pm, Sunday 12pm-8pm

## Why They're a Good Fit
Dalema's premium positioning means:
- Their customers have higher disposable income → more likely to adopt a wallet/loyalty system
- They carry specialty/imported products → inventory tracking and expiry management is critical (imported goods have shorter shelf windows)
- Boutique feel → customer experience is everything, a loyalty program aligns with their brand
- Two locations → multi-branch dashboard adds immediate value
- Bakery/deli → perishable inventory tracking is a daily operational need

## Likely Problems (to confirm during discovery)
Based on similar supermarkets and their premium model:
1. **Inventory waste on perishables** — bakery/deli produces daily, unsold items are waste. Imported items with shorter shelf life expire if not tracked.
2. **No customer retention mechanism** — premium customers visit Dalema for unique items but also shop at Shoprite, Prince Ebeano, and Sahad for staples. No loyalty program to keep them coming back to Dalema first.
3. **Manual inventory** — with imported and specialty items, stock levels and reorder points are harder to manage than standard supermarkets.
4. **No customer data** — they know their customers by face, not by data. No purchase history, no targeted promotions, no "your favourite product is back in stock" messages.
5. **Multi-location coordination** — if both Abuja and Kaduna branches exist, inventory, pricing, and performance need to be visible from one dashboard.

## Solution Approach
Similar to the Kanti Plus model but adapted for premium retail:

### Core Modules
1. **Customer Loyalty Program** — premium-tier rewards that match Dalema's brand positioning. Not just points — exclusive access, first-to-know on new arrivals, bakery preorder, VIP events.
2. **Perishable Inventory Intelligence** — daily tracking for bakery and deli production vs. sales. Waste reduction through production adjustment. Expiry alerts for imported goods.
3. **Smart POS Dashboard** — real-time revenue, product performance, staff metrics, payment breakdown across both locations.
4. **Customer Communication** — SMS/WhatsApp: "Your favourite Italian pasta is back in stock." "New artisan bread this weekend." Targeted to purchase history.
5. **Multi-Branch Dashboard** — Abuja and Kaduna side by side. Compare performance, inventory, customer data.

### Differentiation from Kanti Plus
Kanti Plus is a mass-market supermarket — high volume, competitive pricing.
Dalema is premium — lower volume, higher margin, experience-driven.

The system for Dalema should feel premium:
- Loyalty program should feel exclusive, not transactional
- Communication should feel curated, not mass-blast
- Dashboard should emphasise margin per product and customer lifetime value, not just volume
