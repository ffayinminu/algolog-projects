# 10 Industries × 10 Irresistible Offers
## Grand Slam Offers — Algolog SDS

*Based on: $100M Offers (Alex Hormozi) + Psychology of Selling (Brian Tracy) + Sell or Be Sold (Grant Cardone)*

**The formula:** Stack so much value that saying no feels like losing money. Every offer must pass the test: "Would it be unethical for me NOT to convince this person to buy?" (Cardone conviction test)

**The Value Equation (Hormozi):**
```
              Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
Value = ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
              Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice
```

## Industry 1: Hospitality — Serviced Apartments & Short-Lets

**Product:** Spacer

**The Problem They All Have:**
Manual bookings via WhatsApp, physical key handovers, no-shows they can't prevent, no online presence, revenue leaking through Airbnb's 15% commission.

**The Offer:**
> "We'll deploy a complete booking and property management system for your property — branded booking page, online payments, smart lock access codes that expire at checkout, real-time dashboard, and cart abandonment recovery. Your guests book and pay online. No more WhatsApp. No more keys. No more no-shows."

**What's included:**
- Full Spacer deployment (booking portal + payments + dashboard)
- Smart lock integration (codes auto-generated per booking)
- Auto-generated property marketing website
- Cart abandonment system (reminders to guests who don't complete booking)
- Staff training + user manual
- 30-day operations guarantee

**The kicker:**
> "Every booking that comes through your own portal instead of Airbnb saves you 15% in commission. If you're doing 20 bookings a month at NGN 30,000 average, that's NGN 90,000/month back in your pocket — NGN 1,080,000 a year. The system pays for itself in the first year from commission savings alone."

**Why they can't say no:** The math works. They're already losing the money. The system cost < the annual commission savings. Saying no means choosing to keep losing.

**Why they can't say no:** The cost of ONE disputed balance or ONE reconciliation error can exceed the cost of the system. They're already paying for it in risk — they're just not seeing the invoice.

## How to Use This Document

1. **Before any meeting:** Identify which industry the prospect is in. Read the corresponding offer.
2. **During diagnosis:** Listen for the specific version of "the problem they all have" — then use the kicker with THEIR numbers.
3. **When positioning:** Present the offer as a stack, not a feature list. Everything is included. The value far exceeds the price.
4. **When closing:** Reference the kicker math. "You're already losing NGN [X]. This costs less than what you lose in [timeframe]."
5. **For new industries:** Follow the same template — Problem → Offer → Stack → Kicker → Why they can't say no.
